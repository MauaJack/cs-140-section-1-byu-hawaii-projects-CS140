POSITION

A map is a visual representation of an area, typically depicting geographical features, boundaries, and locations. Maps can represent different scales, from a global map showing continents to a local map highlighting streets. They often use symbols, colors, and labels to convey information, such as roads, rivers, landmarks, and political boundaries. Maps can be physical (printed on paper) or digital (viewable on devices), and they are essential tools for navigation, planning, and understanding spatial relationships between places.
